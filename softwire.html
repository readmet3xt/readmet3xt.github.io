Designing for Speed: The Journey of LNER’s App Clip Ticket Booking at Softwire

How we tackled the stress of last-minute train travel at the station through user-centered design and Apple’s App Clip technology during a summer internship at Softwire.

User-Centered Design App Clip Mobile Design Usability Testing LNER

Introduction

Train travel, while convenient, often comes with the last-minute scramble of ticket booking, especially when you’re already at the station. This common pain point was the focus of our project during my summer internship at Softwire, a leading UK-based software consultancy. Our challenge was to leverage Apple’s then-new App Clip technology to streamline the ticket booking process for LNER (London North Eastern Railway), making it faster and more intuitive for users on the go.

This case study outlines our journey from understanding user needs to designing and prototyping an App Clip solution. My role involved conducting user research, ideation, wireframing, prototyping, and contributing to the overall UX strategy.

The Challenge: The Last-Minute Ticket Scramble

Our initial research, which included observing commuters at train stations and conducting interviews, highlighted several key frustrations:

  • Time Pressure: Users often arrive at the station with limited time before their train departs.
  • App Download Fatigue: Reluctance to download a full app for a one-off or infrequent use.
  • Complex Interfaces: Existing booking processes could be cumbersome, especially on mobile.
  • Payment Friction: Entering payment details under pressure was a significant pain point.

Our problem statement became: How might we enable LNER customers to quickly and seamlessly purchase train tickets at the station, without requiring a full app download?

Our Approach: Leveraging App Clips for Speed and Simplicity

We identified Apple’s App Clip technology as a perfect fit for this challenge. App Clips are small parts of an app that appear when you need them, allowing users to quickly perform a task without downloading the full app. Our approach focused on:

1. User Flow Optimization:

We mapped out the ideal user journey for a last-minute ticket purchase, focusing on minimizing steps and cognitive load. The flow involved scanning an NFC tag or QR code at the station, launching the App Clip, selecting a destination, choosing a ticket type, and completing payment.

2. Minimalist UI Design:

The App Clip’s interface was designed to be clean, intuitive, and focused solely on the task at hand. We prioritized clear calls to action and easy navigation.

3. Seamless Payment Integration:

Leveraging Apple Pay was crucial for reducing payment friction, allowing users to complete transactions with a single tap.

Prototyping & Usability Testing

We created interactive prototypes using Figma and conducted usability testing with target users. Key insights from testing included:

  • Clarity of Information: Users needed clear, concise information about ticket types and prices.
  • Error Prevention: Designing for common errors (e.g., incorrect station selection) was important.
  • Feedback & Confirmation: Clear visual feedback after each step and a strong confirmation of purchase were essential for user confidence.

The Solution: LNER App Clip for Rapid Ticket Booking

Our proposed solution was an LNER App Clip that offered a streamlined, efficient, and user-friendly experience for last-minute train ticket purchases. It addressed the core pain points by:

  • Eliminating App Download: Users could access functionality instantly.
  • Simplifying the Flow: A focused journey from scan to purchase.
  • Enabling Fast Payments: Integration with Apple Pay for quick and secure transactions.
  • Providing Clear Information: Ensuring users had all necessary details at a glance.

Key Learnings & Impact

This project provided valuable insights into designing for specific, time-sensitive user needs and leveraging emerging technologies. Key learnings included:

  • Context is King: Understanding the user’s environment (e.g., a busy train station) is crucial for design decisions.
  • Less is More: For rapid task completion, a minimalist approach to UI and features is highly effective.
  • The Power of New Technologies: App Clips offer a powerful way to deliver just-in-time functionality without the friction of full app downloads.

The LNER App Clip project demonstrated how thoughtful UX design, combined with innovative technology, can significantly improve everyday experiences, making them faster, simpler, and less stressful for users.
