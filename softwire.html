Designing for Speed: LNER’s App Clip

How we tackled the stress of last-minute train travel through user-centered design and Apple’s App Clip technology during a summer internship at Softwire.

User-Centered Design App Clip Mobile Design Usability Testing

The Challenge

Buying a train ticket at the last minute can be stressful. Passengers feel overwhelmed by information and anxious about missing their train. London North Eastern Railway (LNER) wanted a faster, more user-centric way to buy tickets at the station.

Our Approach

Our problem statement was: “How can we enable users to purchase train tickets using an LNER App Clip at the station?” An App Clip is a small, lightweight part of an app that launches instantly for a specific task. We adopted an iterative design process, feeding research insights back into our designs.

The Solution

The result was a focused LNER App Clip booking flow, designed for speed and clarity. The flow included instant access via NFC/QR codes, quick journey planning, clear train options, effortless payment with Apple Pay, and an easily accessible ticket with a QR code and essential journey details.

Results and Learnings

  • Context is King: Designing for a user likely rushing and stressed requires extreme simplification.
  • App Clips Demand Focus: The <10MB limit forces ruthless prioritization. Every interaction must serve the core task efficiently.
  • Iteration Isn’t Optional: Direct user feedback was invaluable in identifying flaws and validating improvements.
