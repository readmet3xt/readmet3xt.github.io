Pebble: Nurturing Workplace Happiness

A Royal College of Art & VISA collaboration on how we designed a virtual companion to combat stress and foster connection for a thriving remote workforce.

Service Design Employee Wellbeing Remote Work Virtual Companion

The Challenge

While remote work offers flexibility, it has also revealed a workforce grappling with stress and isolation. Our research showed a stark decline in employee wellbeing, with 43% rating their mental health as ‘fair’ to ‘very bad’. This wasn’t just about feelings; happier employees are up to 12% more productive. Our brief was to design a service that proactively fosters happiness, transforming it from an HR concern into a core part of the employee experience.

Our Approach

As project lead, I guided our team through an intensive, research-driven design process. We began with surveys and 24 workshops with VISA employees to map emotional journeys and uncover core needs. This data allowed us to develop a key user persona: the "Explorer," an employee aware of their mental status but unsure how to maintain it. Our design process was built around a central question: "How Might We help the Explorer build a foundation of high wellbeing?"

The Solution

Pebble is a virtual assistant integrated into an employee’s workflow. It promotes happiness and productivity through personalized matchmaking, tools for deep work, and a "Virtual Café" to enhance social connection. Pebble acts as a “Happiness Assistant,” gently prompting users to set work-life boundaries and reflect on their achievements.

Results and Learnings

The project resulted in a deeply researched, user-validated concept. Key learnings included:

  • Wellbeing is Not One-Size-Fits-All: Effective solutions must be adaptable and personalizable.
  • Co-creation is Non-Negotiable: Involving users directly was critical to uncovering true needs.
  • Small Nudges Can Create Big Shifts: Gentle prompts for breaks and reflection can significantly influence behavior.
  • The ROI of Happiness is Real: The link between happiness and business metrics makes investing in wellbeing a strategic imperative.
