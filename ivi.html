The Hidden Workload: Improving Women’s Work-Life Balance

An award-winning speculative design project exploring how recognizing 'invisible value' could reshape women's work-life wellbeing by 2040.

Speculative Design Women's Wellbeing Invisible Labor Future of Work

Introduction

The modern working woman often navigates professional ambitions, societal expectations, and the often-unseen, unpaid labor at home. Our project, "A Soul at Work," was a speculative design journey to create the "Invisible Value Income (I.V.I.) Program"—a future-facing concept for 2040 designed to support women's mental health by acknowledging their often-invisible contributions. This exploration was honored in the Speculative Design category at the Core77 Design Awards in 2021.

The Challenge: The Weight of Invisible Burdens

Our initial exploration focused on the multifaceted pressures affecting working women’s mental health. A critical theme was the disproportionate burden of unpaid domestic work and childcare. This "invisible work" is economically undervalued yet crucial for societal function and directly impacts women's time, energy, and career progression.

Our Approach: Deep Empathy and Future-Focused Speculation

We conducted research with 79 working women and 4 wellbeing specialists. Key insights included that stress arises from both external and internal pressures, and a sense of control significantly decreases it. To make these challenges tangible, we developed the persona of "Johanna," a 36-year-old mother juggling remote work and family. We then projected these challenges into a 2040 scenario, asking: "What if invisible value could be evaluated economically?"

The Solution: The Invisible Value Income (I.V.I.) Program

The I.V.I. Program is a speculative governmental public program for 2040. It aims to recognize, measure, and compensate for the "invisible value" created by individuals outside of their formal employment. The program is facilitated through a central digital platform called "Sensei," where users can track their contributions, monitor their wellbeing, and plan their life and work with data-driven reports.

Key Learnings & Impact

This speculative project offered profound learnings:

  • Making the Invisible, Visible: Quantifying unpaid labor can fundamentally shift societal valuations and power dynamics.
  • Wellbeing is Systemic: Individual mental health is deeply intertwined with broader social and economic systems.
  • Speculative Design is a Catalyst for Dialogue: Envisioning a future scenario opens up critical conversations about current inequalities.

The foundational research from this project was utilized by B.C.G. for their internal employee affiliation workshops, demonstrating the immediate relevance of our insights.
