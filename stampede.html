Speeding Up Solutions: Boosting Conservation with Strategic Partnerships

A Royal College of Art project on how we envisioned “Stampede” to forge innovative partnerships and unlock impactful action within the conservation sector.

Conservation Partnerships Design Thinking

The Challenge

The world of wildlife conservation is passionate, yet often faces hurdles. Funding can be difficult to allocate, impactful partnerships can be serendipitous rather than strategic, and connecting the right expertise to the right cause is a persistent challenge. Our research with organizations like WWF revealed that “a lot of money [is] lying around which isn’t being spent” and that many collaborations “form out of luck.”

“There are so many people out there who have similar interests, but connecting them is tough.” — Wildlife Biologist, Founder of Key Conservation

Our Approach

We proposed that Stampede could act as a facilitator within the conservation sector. A core part of our strategy was “Creative Match-Making.” We recognized that different organizations operate with varying levels of Power (influence, resources) and Pace (speed of execution). We developed animal archetypes to make these characteristics relatable, like a high-power, low-pace “Walrus” (WWF) or a low-power, high-pace “Bumblebee” (a startup). Stampede’s goal is to connect partners who complement each other.

Prototyping in Action

To test our methodology, we prototyped a “Stampede” workshop. WWT had expressed that Airbnb was a “dream partner.” We facilitated a 3-hour session with key representatives from both organizations. The session was highly productive and generated significant enthusiasm, proving the value of facilitated collaboration.

“This was 100 times more productive than any partnership meeting I have had.” — Workshop Participant

The Solution

Stampede is a proposed service that identifies pressing causes, conducts strategic matchmaking, designs and facilitates bespoke workshops, and drives actionable outcomes. It operates with a disruptive mindset, a strong network, and a small, agile team focused on rapid action.
